A Mechanism to Produce a Gas with a Specified Oxygen Concentration by Mixing

At the end of September of 2013, I joined the Biological Microsystems Lab at the University of Illinois at Chicago, directed by Dr. Eddington. I stayed in the Biological Microsystems Lab for a little over 2 months. During that time, I was asked to devise a mechanism that could combine gases from different tanks of known oxygen concentrations to produce a mixture with a specified oxygen concentration. The mechanism I devised uses a computer to control the flow of the gases from the input tanks in a pattern that determines the oxygen concentration of the resulting mixture of the input gases. The valves are controlled to enforce a high-frequency pulsatile flow from the input tanks.
